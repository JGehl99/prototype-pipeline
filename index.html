4-month program (2 phases) designed for startups and early-stage businesses that are ready to take their innovative ideas to market

  • Phase 1 – Customer Discovery and Problem-Solution fit The best way to find out what your customers’ pain points are and how you can help them is by talking to them! In this phase, ventures will learn how to gain insight from potential customers (and competition) through hands-on training in survey creation and implementation, interview techniques, and focus groups. Startups will also learn how to analyze their findings and turn that information into actionable adjustments to their proposed idea/invention. These will be the first steps towards their first minimum viable product (MVP) or prototype. .
  • Phase 2 – Prototype Creation and Market Validation In the second phase, ventures will take what they have learned and apply it to the creation and refinement of a prototype/MVP. This might be a 3D printed model, a full-scale mock-up, or a digital rendering. Ventures will also learn how to evaluate their product-market fit by testing their prototype / MVP with potential customers. During this phase, the startups will get guidance from industry members in their field to help them bring their creation from an idea to a tangible product. Participants will have access to the EPIC Marker’s Base as part of this program (Covid-19 regulations permitting)
  • Benefits: opportunity to present their business to a panel of judges at the end of the program for a chance to win a $3,500 cash prize up to $2,500 in funding on eligible business expenses such as:
  • Marketing activities – website design and hosting, print marketing, digital marketing, and business cards
  • Accounting services – bookkeeping, taxes, and technology adoption
  • Legal Services – incorporation registration, and assistance with intellectual property (trademarks, copyright, patents, etc.)
  • Product Development – prototyping, 3D printing, program development, and other technical support services
  • Other Support – additional qualifying expenses at the discretion of the program administration
